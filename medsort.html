Frequently Asked Questions

What is the throughput of MedSort?

MedSort can sort up to 240 medicines in an hour.

How many unused medicines can MedSort accept at a time?

The capacity depends on the medicine’s form factor. For solid unit doses, MedSort can process up to 2,000 medicines in a single sorting cycle.

In how many categories can MedSort sort the medicines?

The number of compartments in MedSort's sorted bins can be reconfigured. MedSort can sort medicines into up to 80–100 distinct categories in a single cycle. If the unsorted pile contains more categories than MedSort can handle, the extras are placed into an overflow bin for the next cycle.

How can MedSort discard expired items when the barcode does not encode the expiration date?

MedSort doesn’t rely solely on barcode data. Using our proprietary vision models, it reads the printed text directly from each medicine, allowing it to extract details not present in the barcode, such as expiration date, and accurately remove expired items.

How much floor space does MedSort occupy?

MedSort occupies roughly 2.5 × 2.5 feet of floor space, about the same footprint as a standard two-door refrigerator.

Does MedSort require network connectivity?

MedSort does not need network connectivity.

How much does MedSort cost?

MedSort is offered under a hardware-as-a-service model. There is a one-time setup fee covering installation and staff training, followed by a monthly subscription fee tailored to your needs. Please contact us at team@inceptionrobotics.ai for pricing.

How does MedSort categorize the medicines?

MedSort’s sorting logic is fully customizable to match each customer’s needs. In most cases, it’s programmed to support restocking for automated dispensing systems such as Carousel, BoxPicker, or PillPick—organizing medicines in a way that reduces restocking time.

What kinds of medicine form factors can MedSort handle?

MedSort can handle unit-dose tablets, custom pill packs, vials, cups, syringes, and other items weighing up to 200 grams.
